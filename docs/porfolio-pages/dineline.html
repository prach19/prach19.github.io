DineLine

Dine Line is a solution to the common university problem: not having enough time to eat. Through multiple rounds of research, my group found that most university students skip meals when they are on campus because the lines are simply too long. Dine Line is an application that displays the estimated time an individual could expect to wait in line for a restaurant on campus, thus helping them find the right place to eat.

Dine Line was created with the Double-Diamond framework, working in an iterative process of conducting preliminary research, creating user personas and profiles, sketching out wireframes, and creating an interactive prototype.
